Effects of Subsidies on Consumer Welfare and Market Structure in the U.S. Broadband Industry

Job Market Paper

This paper studies how public subsidies affect consumer welfare, prices, firm entry, and market structure in the U.S. broadband industry. Broadband subsidies are designed to address two related market failures: affordability constraints faced by low-income households and weak infrastructure deployment incentives in high-cost areas. I combine reduced-form evidence with a structural model of demand, pricing, and entry to quantify the welfare and market-structure effects of demand-side and supply-side subsidy programs.

The analysis compares the short-run affordability effects of household broadband discounts with the longer-run competitive effects of infrastructure subsidies. The results highlight a central policy trade-off: demand-side subsidies can generate large immediate consumer-surplus gains, while supply-side subsidies can expand entry and variety but may deliver lower benefits per dollar when subsidy intensity is too high.

Efficient and Debiased Estimation for Dynamic Discrete Choice Models of Incomplete Information

This paper develops debiased estimation methods for dynamic discrete choice models of incomplete information. The approach treats conditional choice probabilities and transition probabilities as high-dimensional nuisance functions and constructs orthogonal moments that reduce first-step bias. The paper studies how machine learning and cross-fitting can improve estimation in dynamic strategic environments while preserving valid asymptotic inference.

Mobile Money Agent Competition in Senegal

This project studies competition among mobile money operators at the agent level in Senegal. Mobile money agents are a key financial infrastructure in settings where formal banking access remains limited. Using store-level data on operator presence, transactions, and local market characteristics, the project estimates a static entry game to study how rival presence affects profitability and how the entry of a low-cost fintech competitor changes market structure.

Average Monotonicity and the Instability of the Judge IV Estimand

Joint with Mohamed Coulibaly.

This project studies the stability of judge-leniency instrumental variable designs. We examine how the IV estimand can be sensitive to violations of monotonicity and develop diagnostics that quantify how much identifying variation depends on pivotal treatment-response types.
